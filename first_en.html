For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. FIRST.

The FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is a international competition of engineering that involves school teams in more than 45 american states and 9 countries, beyond the engineers of industries e universities. The competition is intended for high school students, the mentors of robots, that work with the teacher assistance, engineers, technicals, private enterprises, universities and comunity peaple interested and believe in the project.

Each team has 6 weeks for design, build and test a robot for accomplish some certain taks. The teams then dispute Reginal Competitions and the Championship. Through this competition, the students practice science and win experience in the engineering cicle of the product development in real time.

In the competition, the robots are built by high school students from a basic kit. The games of the competition happen in the United States, since the year 1992 and seek society recognition for science and tecnology. Beyond this, the FIRST provide to the students, future professional of science area, the participation in a event that aproach the Technologies that normally just are accessible through the internet, TV, and the theater. Comes up of a really army of engineers, students, technicals and teachers that become accomplishers of their dreams, your “heroes” as said the inventor, scientist and co- founder of FIRST, the american Dean Kamen.



Beyond the area of technology, the participation in the FIRST Robotics Competition add either the participation of high school students, academics, and professionals in the area of marketing, logistics, public relations and administration , witch are fundamental for the teams participation in the competition.

In the beginning of each year, in January, is revealed the game and the rules of the competition, through the points through satellites conected via TV of NASA. It should be noted that the game and the rules of competition change each year. The team Under Control of Marista Pio XII college was the second brazilian team to participate in the competition, fact that happened in the year of 2003.
