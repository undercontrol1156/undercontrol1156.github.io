Under Control in the Community.

We want our community to have the same educational opportunities that we had. This way, we make 630 hours of community service annually. Monthly, 1156 takes science and technology, through Mindstorm, VEX and FRC robots to minorities.



Once a week, we take our kits of technological education to the public nursery of Regina Hospital and present to the children what FIRST taught us. The director of the nursery said: "The students of 1156 add a lot to the lives and education of these children. They all have moments of distraction and fun, something that contributes a lot to their recovery. Without 1156, they wouldn't know robotics and all knowledge that comes with it." Since our social work in the hospital, we inspired the institution to create their own volunteering program, in which we volunteered as "actors".



Monthly, we took robotics to young children of APAE, a school for intellectually challenged children, and to the Institute of Children Cancer. We evidence their growth and personal discoveries, progressively learning and knowing the pieces, the little robots and having engineering ideas that teaches us as much we teach them.



When we come to these institutions, we realize the children's joy in their smiles. In 2013 we made a whole new project, which we take to all social activities we host. This project spread FIRST and its games: each child play a game where he/she should match pieces with images of robots and the objectives of the last 12 years of FRC games. After this, they build a Mindstorm robot that should overcome a challenge, inspired in FRC's. Our game, Find&Match is a differential in these children educations.
